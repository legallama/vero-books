Migrating from QuickBooks Online

Learn how to seamlessly transfer your historical data, chart of accounts, and contacts from QuickBooks Online to VeroBooks using our official integration.

Overview

VeroBooks provides a dedicated Migration Assistant that connects directly to your Intuit QuickBooks Online account via OAuth2. This ensures your financial data is imported securely and accurately without needing to format messy CSV files.

Step 1: Obtain Your Intuit API Credentials

Before connecting, you need to create an app in the Intuit Developer Portal:

  1. Go to developer.intuit.com and sign in.
  2. Navigate to Dashboard > Create an App and choose "QuickBooks Online and Payments".
  3. Select the Keys & Credentials tab on the left sidebar.
  4. Copy your Client ID and Client Secret.

Step 2: Configure the Migration Assistant

Now, enter your credentials into VeroBooks:

  1. In VeroBooks, click on QBO Migration in the left sidebar (under Developer & API).
  2. Paste your Client ID and Client Secret into the Connection Settings panel.
  3. Ensure the environment is set to Production (unless you are testing with a developer sandbox).
  4. Click Save Settings.

Step 3: Connect and Authorize

Once your keys are saved, click the green Connect to QuickBooks button. You will be securely redirected to Intuit to log in and authorize VeroBooks to read your accounting data. Once authorized, you will be redirected back to VeroBooks, and your status will change to "Connected".

Step 4: Sync Your Data

With an active connection, you can now sync your data modules:

  • Chart of Accounts: Imports all your active GL accounts to ensure your books map correctly. Start here.
  • Customers & Vendors: Brings over all your CRM data, including billing addresses and contact information.
  • Products & Inventory: Syncs your catalog of items and services.
  • Historical Transactions: Imports past invoices, bills, and journal entries to populate your ledger history.

Important: We recommend running a Trial Balance report in QuickBooks before migrating, and comparing it against your VeroBooks Trial Balance after the migration to ensure everything synced correctly.
